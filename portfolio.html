Client Century 21 Canada

Project Website & Customer Journey Redesign
The Challenge

The existing website presented every visitor with the same experience regardless of their goals.

Key trust signals were buried. Calls-to-action were difficult to discover. The homepage functioned more like a brochure than a lead generation tool.

Our Diagnosis

Instead of asking,

"How can we redesign this?"

We asked,

"What is preventing this website from becoming a stronger customer acquisition system?"

Our review identified four major opportunities:

  • Lack of personalization
  • Hidden conversion opportunities
  • Weak trust positioning
  • High visitor friction
The Strategy

Rather than making cosmetic improvements, we redesigned the customer journey.

1

Created clearer decision paths for different visitor types

2

Strengthened trust signals above the fold

3

Reduced friction throughout the experience

4

Improved conversion opportunities at every stage

5

Designed every interaction around visitor intent

What Changed

Instead of listing dozens of features, here are the changes that matter most:

  • Personalized homepage experience
  • Simplified property search
  • Persistent lead capture
  • Stronger trust positioning
  • Interactive mortgage calculator
  • Optimized mobile experience
Business Impact

Not "we added features." Here's what actually changed for the business:

Visitors can now identify the path that's relevant to them within seconds.

Potential sellers reach the valuation tool faster.

Trust is established earlier in the journey.

Lead opportunities exist throughout the page instead of only at the end.

The customer journey now removes hesitation instead of creating it.

Every redesign should answer one question:

"How does this improve business performance?"

Insights From This Project

What We Learned

Every project teaches us something. Here's what this one reinforced.

Lesson #1

Context matters more than data.

People don't trust statistics. They trust statistics presented in the right context. Numbers alone don't persuade — the story around them does.

Lesson #2

Fewer choices, better engagement.

Giving visitors fewer, clearer choices often improves engagement more than offering every option at once. Clarity beats comprehensiveness.

Lesson #3

Personalization is about recognition.

Personalization isn't about technology. It's about helping people immediately recognize that they're in the right place.
